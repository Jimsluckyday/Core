Capper leaderboard — internal, not public

Ranked purely by win % once a capper clears the minimum — total pick count never drives the ranking, it's only shown as context. Default minimum is 2 graded picks, since a single pick isn't a meaningful percentage. Results open in a new tab so they don't interrupt data entry. "All time" clears filters and runs immediately — the typical view.

Capper performance by bet type — internal, not public

Each row is one capper's record within a single bet type (e.g. "King Cap · Spread"), not their overall record — so a capper who's mediocre overall can still show up strong on one bet type. Sorted by win % within each qualifying row, same minimum-picks logic as the leaderboard. Use this to decide which of a capper's bet types are actually worth publishing, rather than promoting or hiding a capper wholesale.

Pick tier performance — internal, not public

Breaks down every graded pick by tier — count, win-loss-push, and win % for Tier 0 (base picks) through Tier 4 (a capper's highest-confidence pick, however they brand it — Whale of the Day, Lock of the Week, VIP pick, etc.). Also breaks it down per capper, since cappers use different tiers differently, and one capper's Tier 4 may perform very differently than another's.

Consensus picks — internal, not public yet

A consensus requires the exact same selection AND line (where applicable) — "Spurs -6" and "Spurs -6.5" are counted separately, on purpose. Uncheck the box above to see every qualifying consensus for deeper internal review — the default shows just the strongest one per day, which is the version worth showing end users later.

Publish ranking — internal, not public

Scores every currently-pending pick using four equally-weighted signals (25% each): win % in this exact sport + bet type combo (e.g. Tennis Spreads specifically, not Spreads in general), overall win %, current streak (a bad streak actively drags the score down, not just withholds a bonus), and confluence — a bonus or PENALTY when other cappers independently picked this exact same game/selection/line. An agreeing capper above a 50% record helps the score; one below 50% actively hurts it, and the effect grows stronger both with a larger losing sample and with more agreeing cappers (capped at 3+). The sport+bet-type rate blends toward that capper's broader all-sport rate for that bet type until enough sport-specific picks pile up — so "2-0 in Tennis Spreads" leans on their overall Spread record at first, rather than either treating 2-0 as proven or ignoring it as meaningless. All win percentages are otherwise sample-size-adjusted the same way — a lucky small sample can't out-rank a proven track record. Click the small "N agreeing ▾" note under any score to see exactly which other cappers contributed to that pick's confluence bonus. Sort by Score to find the best candidates, then click Publish on however many you want featured.

All published picks

Shows every published pick regardless of result — Publish Ranking only ever looks at still-pending picks, so a published pick disappears from that view the moment it's graded, even though it's still live on the public site. This is the place to see everything you've ever published, win/loss/push included, all in one list. Export to CSV for anything you need outside the database or website itself (presentations, investor materials, etc.).

Publish performance — internal, not public

Every pick published since the score-tracking feature was added has its score snapshotted at the exact moment you clicked Publish — this compares that snapshot against the actual graded result, bucketed by score range, so you can see whether a high score has actually meant a higher win rate in practice, or whether the formula needs recalibrating. Only picks with both a score snapshot AND a final graded result (win/loss/push) count toward the win % — still-pending ones are shown separately, not counted either way yet. Picks published *before* score tracking existed have no snapshot to compare — they're listed in their own section at the bottom for completeness, but can't contribute to the win % analysis since there's nothing to check the outcome against.

Formula backtest — internal, not public

Recalculates what score EVERY graded pick would have gotten, using only the data that actually existed before that pick happened — not each capper's full current record, which would unfairly use information from the future. This is the real test of whether the formula works: if higher scores actually produced higher win rates historically, the weighting is doing its job; if the buckets come out flat, that's a sign the formula (or its weights) needs a second look. Also breaks down each of the four components on its own, so you can see which ones are actually predictive versus just adding noise.

Formula settings — internal, not public

⚠ This changes how the formula scores picks GOING FORWARD only. It can never affect anything already published — every published pick's score and Hot Pick status were permanently locked in the moment you clicked Publish, and nothing here can reach back and change that. The public-facing page also never runs this formula at all, so end users will never see any sign that a change was made. Even so: run a backtest first, only change one thing at a time, and confirm the change actually helps before trusting it.

Other tunable values

Pick tier bonuses (flat points, Tier 0 is always 0)

Smoothing (k) controls how fast a capper's win % is trusted as sample size grows — higher means more proof required before trusting a small sample. Streak cap is the longest streak that still adds extra weight beyond it (a 25-game streak scores the same as one right at the cap). Confluence cap is how many agreeing cappers it takes to reach full confluence weight. Hot Pick threshold is the win-streak length required to earn the public "Hot Pick" badge at the moment a pick is published. Tier bonuses are flat points added to a pick's score based on which tier the capper marked it as (Tier 0 never gets a bonus) — each tier defaults to 0 (no effect) independently; check the Formula Backtest's tier breakdown first to see whether the real data actually supports setting a given tier above 0, since one tier being worth it doesn't mean they all are.

Top & bottom cappers — internal, not public yet

Top 5 and Bottom 5 shown as separate lists, ranked purely by win %. Default minimum is 2 graded picks — a single pick is too small a sample to mean anything (100% off one pick isn't a real record), but 2+ gets ranked on percentage alone. Raise the minimum if you want a stricter bar. Value column estimates $100-per-pick profit using whichever odds are on file (opening if present, otherwise closing) — picks with no odds logged are excluded from that total, so it's a partial number until odds entry is more complete.

Capper streaks — internal, not public yet

Shows every capper currently on a win or loss streak at or above the minimum length — separate from win % or ranking, so a capper can show up here even if their overall record looks fine. Streak counts consecutive picks (a push doesn't break it), most recent first.

Capper analysis — internal, not public yet

"Total" counts every pick as its own data point. "Unique" collapses picks that are the exact same bet on the exact same game (same date, sport, bet type, selection, and line) down to one — so if five cappers all picked "Chiefs -6," that's 5 total picks but 1 unique bet. Also broken down by sport and by bet type, so you can see where accuracy is actually strongest, not just overall.

Duplicate pick checker — internal, not public

Finds picks that are an exact match on capper, date, sport, bet type, selection, line, and (for props) player/stat — the kind of thing that happens when the same file gets bulk-imported twice, or two people enter the same capper's picks without realizing it. Review each group and delete the extra copy — the original stays untouched, this never deletes automatically.

Undo grading — internal, testing use only

Resets graded picks (win/loss/push) back to pending, for whichever capper/sport/date range you set above — meant for undoing a wrong-date grading run during testing, not everyday use. This shows you exactly which picks would be affected first; nothing changes until you explicitly confirm. If any of these picks are currently published, resetting them will make that result disappear from the public "past days" view until they're graded again.

Undo schedule sync — internal, testing use only

Clears start time, home/away, and closing odds (and the sync status flag) for whichever capper/sport/date range you set above — meant for undoing a Sync/Backfill run against the wrong date, not everyday use. This only clears what the sync tools themselves write; it never touches opening odds or anything you entered by hand. Shows you exactly which picks would be affected first — nothing changes until you explicitly confirm.

Grading exceptions — picks the auto-grader couldn't resolve

With a specific date entered: shows every pick still pending for that exact day only — since you already ran the grader on that day, anything still pending is by definition something it couldn't resolve. Working one day at a time keeps this from mixing multiple test days together into one confusing batch. Leave the date blank to instead search broadly across every day whose event date has already passed, plus anything the grader explicitly flagged as ambiguous — useful for an occasional wider sweep, not everyday use. Checking the box above catches problems earlier still — right after you run "Sync Schedule & Odds" in Pending Picks, any pick it couldn't confidently match gets included here too, whether or not its event date has passed yet, so a wrong date or unclear detail can get fixed well before grading day instead of showing up as a surprise later. Export these to CSV, work through them (in this chat, a fresh one, or however you research them), then use "Grading Corrections" below to bulk-apply what you find.

Flagged lines — spot-check whenever you have time, never blocks anything

These are picks where research noticed a line that looked like a possible transcription error, not just normal line movement (which is expected and never flagged). Grading already happened normally against the originally entered line regardless -- this list is purely for an optional later look, on your own time, to confirm it really was what the capper sent. Clearing a flag doesn't change anything about the pick itself.

Data review queue — fix incomplete picks one at a time

Uncheck anything you've decided isn't worth chasing right now — for example, leave "Opening odds" unchecked during testing if you've already decided historical opening odds aren't realistically recoverable, and a pick only missing that won't show up in the queue at all. Once started, each pick is shown one at a time with just the missing pieces to fill in — Save & Next commits your changes and moves on, Skip leaves it untouched and moves on, and the counter always shows how many are left.

Odds exceptions — picks missing odds, regardless of grading status

Unlike Grading Exceptions above (which only looks at still-pending picks), this checks EVERY pick regardless of result — a pick can already be graded win/loss/push and still be missing odds entirely, which matters just as much for published data integrity. Export to CSV, research the approximate odds (an AI chat search, Covers, Flashscore, etc. all work fine — this is for historical backfill, so exact precision isn't required), then paste the results into Grading Corrections below using just id and opening_odds/closing_odds — no need to touch result at all if grading is already correct.

Grading corrections — bulk-apply researched results back onto exact picks

Paste rows copied from a spreadsheet (or built in a chat) — first row must be column headers. Required: id only. Optional: result, event_date, event_name, line, opening_odds, closing_odds, line_flag, notes — only included columns get changed; anything left out is left alone. If result is included it must be exactly win, loss, or push, and marks the pick as manually graded — leave it blank entirely for an odds-only correction on a pick that's already graded (notes in that case are saved to the pick's own notes field instead, since grading wasn't touched). The line a capper gave us is always what we grade against, regardless of normal line movement between when the pick was sent and when it was researched — that's expected and never needs fixing. line should only ever be used for a genuine, obvious transcription error, and is rare; for the much more common "the line looks different than current research" situation, use line_flag (enter yes) instead — it doesn't change anything about the pick or hold up grading, it just adds it to the Flagged Lines list in Data Tools for an optional, unhurried look later. opening_odds / closing_odds are meant for a quick manual lookup (an AI chat search, Covers, Flashscore, or similar) when a pick's sport isn't covered by our own odds API, or historical odds are missing entirely — approximate is fine here, and if that pick is already published, this will correctly flow into the site's live win-rate and $100-per-bet totals the moment it's saved. Matching is by the exact pick id, never by name/date guessing, so there's no risk of updating the wrong pick.

Bulk import from spreadsheet

Paste rows copied from a spreadsheet (or built here in chat) — first row must be column headers. Required columns: date, capper, sport, bet type, selection. Optional: line, home_away, odds, prop_player, prop_stat (or just "stat"), confidence, notes, published. For player props, if you don't include a separate prop_player column, the player's name is automatically taken from selection. Capper/sport/bet type must already exist in this tool (added via the Cappers/Sports/Bet Types section above) — unmatched names get flagged instead of guessed at.
